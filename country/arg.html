Findings

The Community trust in the Argentine Red Cross can vary among different population groups, including volunteers, beneficiaries, and the general population.

Volunteers

Volunteers are individuals who actively participate in various Argentina Red Cross programs, initiatives, or projects.

People receiving support

Individuals who directly receive benefits or support from Argentina Red Cross programs, such as social welfare, healthcare, or education initiatives

Others

Individual who do not receive benefits or support from Argentina Red Cross programs or engaged with the Argentina Red Cross in general

Perceptions of community trust

It's important to note that community trust in the Red Cross is a complex and multifaceted concept. It is influenced by a range of factors beyond the population groups above, such as competencies or ethical values. Analysis

Argentina Trust index shows a high level of trust from community. However, we observed a relatively low perception of receptiveness regarding other competencies. This low perception could affect trust in Red Cross.

Recommendations:

To address a low perception of receptiveness, it's crucial to actively demonstrate receptiveness and create an environment that encourages open dialogue, values diverse perspectives, and appreciates constructive feedback.
  • actively listening and demonstrating empathy
  • responding thoughtfully to concerns,
  • taking appropriate actions based on the feedback received,
  • Share with community how you addressed their feedbacks
