Trust is criticalfor achieving impact inRed Cross and Red Crescent programsand operations.It is a critical determinant of behaviours.With trust, communities can acceptlifesaving information, accept and uptake recommendedmeasures, provide feedback we need toact upon,and participate in decisions affecting them. Often taken for granted, trust is not static and must be constantly earned and maintained.It is driven by a solid social contract, which are rules, obligations, norms that bind different people together and influence how they act and behave.

How to Build Community Trust

Fostering community trust, social cohesion and civil responsibility through the active engagement of communities is a necessary condition to successfully deliver impact, build resilience and sustainability.

Building community trust though is complex and requires systematic understanding of trust variation and the social, economic, cultural, and environmental factors that influence trust. It requires understanding the root causes of mistrust and the local dynamics of trust.

Without a tool to measure the different dimensions of trust among providers and receivers of services, it is hard for public health and humanitarian responders to take action and create strategies to rebuild and maintain community trust.

What is the Community Trust Index?

The IFRC Community Trust Index is a standardised tool for measuring set of survey questions that together measure the level of community trust toward a humanitarian aid organisation such as a Red Cross and Red Crescent Society. It is a multidimesional tool that measures trust through trust in competency and trust in values.

Why Measuring Trust is Critical for National Societies?

By implementing the Community Trust Index, National Societies can:

  • Measure the dimensions of trust and track variation over time.
  • Explore the factors that influence trust in specific communities and populations they serve.
  • Develop evidence-based recommendations, plans, and actions to increase community trust.
  • Address the social, economic, cultural, and environmental factors of trust andadvocate towards increase compliance, better governance, and accountability to communities.
  • Reinforce agency at the donor level and strengthen partnerships for targeted investment in priority areas.
